## Decision

Use the current chassis/suspension/brake access window to open and inspect the rear differential and axle center section before final chassis coating, suspension closeout, brake closeout, and road validation.

The attached photo shows the differential carrier / axle pumpkin area with the cover/case fasteners visible. Treat this as a condition gate, not a cosmetic task. Do not clean and paint over this assembly until oil condition, breather condition, axle seals, bearing play, carrier sealing, brake-line routing, and hub/drum interfaces have been checked.

If the workshop confirms the photo is the front axle instead, mirror the same inspection logic to that axle and update the labels before disassembly.

## Scope

Open enough of the rear axle assembly to answer these questions:

| Question | Required answer |
| --- | --- |
| Is the oil clean, correct, and free of water/metal? | Drain, inspect, photograph, and log findings before refill. |
| Is the carrier/third-member sealing face dry and intact? | Inspect the visible fastener ring, mating face, studs/nuts, and old sealant/gasket condition. |
| Are the ring and pinion usable? | Inspect tooth faces, contact pattern, backlash indication, pitting, chips, scoring, and abnormal wear. |
| Are the carrier and pinion bearings healthy? | Check for roughness, play, overheating discoloration, metal debris, or noise. |
| Are axle shafts, splines, wheel bearings, and axle seals healthy? | Inspect during rear drum/brake work while shafts/hubs are accessible. |
| Is the axle housing straight and serviceable? | Check tubes, spring pads, shock mounts, breather, drain/fill plugs, and obvious impact damage. |
| Does brake/suspension routing remain safe after reassembly? | Recheck rear hard lines, center flex hose, parking-brake cables, breather hose, shocks, U-bolts, and leaf-spring clearance. |

1. Photograph and label the axle before cleaning.

3. Drain oil and inspect oil/magnet findings before suspension/brake parts are removed.

## Teardown Sequence

1. Mark the axle as `rear_differential_axle_inspection_20260517`; label left/right on every removed shaft, drum, backing-plate part, shim, washer, and fastener group.
2. Photograph the assembly exactly as found, including current dirt/oil patterns. Leak patterns matter.
3. Crack the fill plug loose before draining. If the fill plug is seized, stop and solve that first.
4. Drain oil into a clean pan. Photograph the first oil, final oil, drain plug, magnet, and any sludge, water, or metallic debris.
5. Degrease the outside after the oil/leak evidence is captured. Avoid forcing water/solvent into the breather, pinion seal, hub seals, or open brake parts.
6. Inspect the housing externally: tubes, welds, spring pads, shock mounts, U-bolt seats, brake-line tabs, breather fitting, drain/fill bosses, and carrier fastener ring.
7. Open rear drums as part of the brake baseline. Keep shoe/hardware layout photos before removing springs or adjusters.
8. Remove axle shafts/hubs only using the correct axle procedure. Keep side labels and do not mix shims, retainers, or bearing parts.
9. If the carrier/third member is removed, support it properly and keep dirt away from the open housing. Do not pry on sealing faces.
10. Inspect the ring gear, pinion, carrier bearings, pinion bearing indication, spider/side gears, thrust washers, fasteners, and carrier case.
11. Use gear marking compound and a dial indicator to record contact pattern and backlash indication. Exact accept/reject values must come from the Toyota/manual sheet for the fitted axle.
12. Clean the housing interior only after all debris evidence is photographed. Remove sludge, old gasket/sealant, and loose contamination.
13. Decide the repair path before reassembly.

## Decision Matrix

| Finding | Action |
| --- | --- |
| Clean oil, no metal, good teeth, no roughness/play, dry seals | Reseal only where opened, clean breather, refill correct oil, and move to validation. |
| Dirty oil, minor sludge, blocked breather, light carrier seep, no gear damage | Clean, reseal carrier, service breather, replace plug washers/sealant, refill, and recheck for leaks. |
| Axle seal leak, contaminated drum, bearing roughness, or shaft seal-land damage | Replace affected seal/bearing/hardware before brake closeout; clean or replace contaminated brake friction parts. |
| Pinion seal leak but no bearing/gear fault | Replace pinion seal only if the shop can control flange/yoke marking and preload procedure exactly. |
| Metallic glitter/chunks, water in oil, chipped/pitted gears, blue heat marks, rough bearings, backlash clearly out, or tooth pattern abnormal | Stop cosmetic work. Send carrier/axle to a differential specialist or source a known-good/rebuildable assembly. |
| Bent housing tube, cracked spring pad, damaged shock mount, stripped carrier studs, or damaged fill/drain bosses | Repair housing before suspension alignment or coating. |

## Reassembly Rules

- Use the Toyota/manual torque sheet for carrier nuts, bearing caps, flange/yoke fasteners, axle retainers, brake backing hardware, wheel fasteners, and any disturbed suspension brackets.
- Do not disturb ring/pinion setup unless the shop has the gauges and experience to reset preload, backlash, and contact pattern.
- Replace lock tabs, cotter pins, distorted washers, damaged nuts, and single-use hardware where the manual calls for it.
- Use only approved carrier gasket or sealant on clean, dry sealing faces. Do not over-apply sealant into the housing.
- Fill with the correct gear oil only after confirming whether the diff is open or limited slip.
- Clear and route the breather so pressure does not force oil past new seals.
- Recheck rear brake line, parking-brake cable, breather, shock, U-bolt, and leaf-spring clearance at static height and supported droop before road use.

## No-Go Conditions

- Fill plug cannot be removed.
- No safe support setup or axle support.
- No caps/plugs for any brake hydraulic line opened during the job.
- No manual/spec sheet for torque and gear measurement values.
- Shop wants to remove the carrier, bearings, pinion, or ring gear without marking parts and recording measurements.
- Brake shoes/drums are contaminated with gear oil and are still planned for reuse without cleaning/replacement decision.
- Chassis/axle coating is planned before leak sources and breather condition are closed.
