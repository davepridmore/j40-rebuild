## Current Condition Read

The chassis is not ready for primer yet, but it is now far enough exposed to run a proper closeout pass. The large outside rail faces have been brushed in many areas and the remaining corrosion visible in the photos looks mostly like surface rust, old coating edges, and rust trapped around brackets/weld toes rather than obvious frame perforation.

This is not a structural sign-off. The photos do not show every inner rail face, top flange, hidden bracket backside, or the metal under hard-line clips. The final decision has to be made after degreasing, drying, close light inspection, and probing.

## Brushing Acceptance Criteria

Before primer or coating, each approved zone should meet all of these:

- No loose rust scale, loose black paint, mud, oil film, or sanding dust remains.
- Existing paint can stay only where it is hard-bonded and feathered at the edge.
- Rust in pits is mechanically reduced as far as practical without thinning the frame.
- Weld toes, bolt holes, spring hanger roots, body-mount pads, crossmember ends, and line-clip contact areas have close-up photos after cleaning.
- Hard-line clips are checked underneath, not only around the outside edge.
- Any soft metal, crack line, ovalized hanger hole, or deep pitting is logged before coating.

## Chassis Next Sequence

1. Dry clean first:
   - Blow/vacuum dust and loose debris.
   - Scrape heavy deposits before wet cleaning.
   - Mask or lift vulnerable electrical connectors and open line ends.
2. Detail brushing:
   - Wire cup on bracket roots, curved areas, weld toes, holes, and edges.
   - Strip disc or flap disc only on flatter faces where control is good. These are angle-grinder consumables; existing 125 mm flap discs and nylon fiber stripping/polishing discs are already recorded, so count physical stock before rebuying.
   - Hand wire brush around hard lines, clips, and body-mount holes.
3. Degrease:
   - Use the received `DISS (APC) All Purpose Cleaner 5L` for broad grime.
   - Use received `GREZ OFF HD Degreaser` only on heavier oil/grease zones.
   - The ordered `Wadfow Pressure sprayer WRS1550` is the registered tool for applying water-based degreaser.
4. Rinse and dry:
   - Rinse with controlled pressure, not a close-range blast into seams, bearings, breathers, electrics, or line ends.
   - Dry with compressed air or blower, then leave open until fully dry.
   - Do not apply primer the same day if seams or boxed areas are still wet.
5. Post-clean inspection:
   - Bright light inspection, screwdriver/pick probing, and close-up photos.
   - Mark repair/no-repair decisions on the chassis with tape or paint marker.
6. Rust treatment:
   - Use Evapo-Rust only where parts can stay wet/immersed or be wrapped properly.
   - Use a compatible rust converter only in remaining pits and seams where mechanical removal cannot fully reach.
   - Do not convert clean bare steel unnecessarily.
7. Rust-treatment closeout and solvent wipe:
   - Let converter fully cure, then remove or neutralize converter residue exactly as that product requires.
   - Confirm the chassis is cool, dry, and free of sanding dust before solvent wiping.
   - Use wax and grease remover before primer only after rust treatment has fully cured and residue is removed.
   - Allow full solvent flash-off before masking and primer.
8. Masking:
   - Mask threads, ground pads, drain holes, brake/fuel fittings, line contact areas, rubber contact faces, and holes that must stay open. Use sacrificial bolts for threaded holes; use the on-hand grommet pack only for open unthreaded holes where the grommet fits snugly and passes a quick solvent/rubber-residue test.
9. Primer:
   - Prefer a two-part epoxy primer for cleaned chassis metal.
   - If self-etching primer is used anywhere, confirm it is compatible with the epoxy/topcoat system. Do not stack incompatible self-etch and epoxy products.
10. Seams and edges:
   - Apply seam sealer only after primer where joints need sealing and where the product system calls for it.
   - Do not seal over uninspected rust or wet seams.
11. Topcoat and cavity protection:
   - Apply the on-hand U-POL/Raptor protective coating as the exposed chassis top protection over cured primer/sealer.
   - Do not add a chassis-black layer under Raptor unless the exact product data confirms the cure, scuff, and recoat compatibility.
   - Use HB Body U900 cavity wax spray cans last inside boxed/closed sections and hidden pockets with the wand/nozzle.

## Engine And Powertrain Cleaning Task

The engine photos show a useful cleaning baseline: dust, oil staining, gearbox/transfer grime, and exposed line/linkage detail. Clean this as a separate mechanical task before judging leaks or ordering extra engine parts.

1. Preparation:
   - Engine cold.
   - Cover intake openings, alternator, starter, exposed connectors, fuse/relay areas, open breathers, and loose electrical ends.
   - Keep degreaser away from labels, old rubber left in place for identification, and open fluid ports.
2. Degrease:
   - Use DISS/APC for general dirt.
   - Use GREZ OFF only on oily bellhousing/gearbox/transfer/engine-side deposits.
   - Agitate with brushes before rinsing.
3. Controlled rinse:
   - Use low to medium pressure and distance.
   - Do not blast injection pump seals, injector lines, alternator, starter, gearbox/axle breathers, electrical connectors, or bearing/seal edges.
4. Dry:
   - Blow out water from linkages, bolt pockets, connectors, and case ribs.
   - Leave open to dry before reconnecting or starting anything.
5. Inspection after dry:
   - Photograph oil leak sources before wiping again.
   - Check fuel return/leak-off hoses, coolant hoses, vacuum hoses, clamps, engine mounts, gearbox mounts, clutch linkage, and ground straps.
   - Do not paint engine or transmission surfaces until leak sources and hose replacements are resolved.
