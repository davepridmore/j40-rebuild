## Scope

This covers the paired off-vehicle vent/quarter window assemblies shown in the May 3 intake photo. They are not a loose trim cleanup item; they belong in the body/weather-seal lane because they must be mechanically serviceable and water-controlled before final body refit.

## Workflow Position

1. `WP01` closes body rust, sealing faces, and paint contact areas.
2. `WP02` refurbishes the vent/quarter window assemblies on the bench.
3. `WP02` releases them only after seals, pivots/latches, glass fit, and water path are checked.
4. `WP06` installs them during final reassembly and validation.

## Execution Rules

- Tag LH/RH assemblies and bag all small hardware before any teardown.
- Photograph both sides, pivots/latches, tabs, rubber profile, and glass markings before disassembly.
- Do not buy seals, glass, or mechanisms until teardown decides reusable versus replace.
- Treat rusted tabs/channels as small metal refinish work: clean, de-rust, epoxy-prime, and topcoat before rubber/glass goes back.
- Keep glass protected during rust work and do not force hardened rubber around the glass edge.
- Bench-check latch movement and run a controlled water test before the parts are marked refit-ready.

## Closure Gate

Close `front_vent_window_assemblies` only when each assembly has:

- side/orientation tags and hardware bags attached;
- clean/refinished metal channels and tabs;
- smooth latch/pivot movement;
- reusable seals confirmed or replacement seal profile measured and sourced;
- glass cleaned and checked for chips/cracks;
- bench fit and water-test result recorded.
