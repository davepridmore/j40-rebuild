# Hose Local Market Order Sheet - 2026-05-04

Purpose: one sheet to send to a local hose/rubber/hydraulic market for the Toyota Land Cruiser J40/HJ47 with Toyota 2H diesel. The lengths below are the buy lengths and include units. Final trimming is done on the vehicle unless the item is a molded hose or a complete hydraulic assembly.

Scout position: quote new replacement components only. Old hoses, hard lines, clips, and hydraulic assemblies are samples for length/shape/fitting match, not parts to reuse or buy used.

Detailed engineering controls stay in [engine-hose-tube-replacement-specs.md](engine-hose-tube-replacement-specs.md) and [replacement-pipes-workstream.md](replacement-pipes-workstream.md).

For a component-by-component market pack that also includes chassis rubbers, compact fuse protection, and the brake booster decision, use [Local Market Component Order Spec](local-market-component-order-spec-20260504.md).

## Message To Shop

Please supply or quote the exact new items below for a Toyota Land Cruiser J40/HJ47 with 2H diesel. Use automotive-rated hose only. Send photos of hose printing, packaging, end fittings, clamp style, and price before payment.

Brake and clutch flex hoses must be new complete crimped hydraulic assemblies copied from the old assembly or matched to the current vehicle fittings. Do not supply generic rubber hose for brake or clutch hydraulics.

For shopping, use the listed length and material/rating. Shaping tools are separate workshop support: molded radiator hoses are bought as molded shapes, rubber hose stock is cut/routed on the vehicle, and only metal hard lines need a cutter/bender/flaring kit if fabricated at home.

## Hard Rules

- No clear PVC, washer hose, or unmarked soft hose for fuel.
- No used hoses, used hydraulic assemblies, used hard lines, or used clamps for this scout list.
- No generic rubber hose for brake or clutch hydraulics.
- No sharp perforated worm clamps on diesel hoses.
- No fuel hose as a substitute for radiator or heater hose.
- No coolant-only hose for oil breather or oil-mist routes.
- No bare copper for brake or fuel hard lines.
- Do not coat the formed coolant pipe until it passes dry-fit and pressure test.
