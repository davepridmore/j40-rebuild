# Left Chassis Extension Repair Plan - 2026-05-17

Evidence:

- User attachments on 2026-05-17 showing the left chassis extension with a rough lower-side cut-out.

## Decision

Treat this as a `P0` chassis repair hold before final primer, seam sealer, Raptor, or cavity wax.

The visible cut-out looks like a non-factory clearance notch, possibly made for an old exhaust route. The repair target is to restore the chassis extension to its original closed lower-side profile, then route the exhaust around the restored rail rather than cutting the frame again.

Do not cover this with filler, seam sealer, Raptor, or an external cosmetic plate. Repair metal first, validate geometry, then coat.

## Photo Read

- The left rail/extension has a rough semicircular section missing from the lower side wall.
- The cut edge is jagged and oxidized, which suggests previous modification rather than a clean factory relief.
- Adjacent bracketry, body-mount/crossmember structure, and rail fasteners are still present, so the missing section should be repaired in context rather than replaced by a generic flat patch.
- Nearby exhaust or hanger planning must be reopened because the old clearance path should not remain the design driver.

## Repair Principle

Restore the rail profile from measured evidence, not from guesswork.

Primary geometry source:

1. Mirror the intact opposite-side chassis extension profile if it is sound and unmodified.
2. Use fixed datums visible on both sides: rivets/bolts, bracket edges, body-mount pedestal, rail top edge, flange crease, and nearby crossmember/bracket junctions.
3. Confirm with any factory chassis drawing or known good reference only if available.

If the opposite side has different bracket details, copy the base rail/extension wall and lower flange shape only, not unrelated bracket features.

## Measurement And Inspection Gate

Before cutting or welding:

1. Degrease and wire-brush at least `75 mm` around the cut-out on both sides of the rail wall.
2. Photograph the repaired zone-to-be square-on, from below, and from inside/behind if accessible.
3. Probe the cut edge and adjacent lower flange for thin metal, laminating rust, cracks, and old weld repairs.
4. Measure actual chassis metal thickness with calipers where the edge is clean.
5. Make a cardboard profile template from the intact opposite side.
6. Transfer the template to the damaged side and mark the original rail outline in paint marker.
7. Check rail straightness and symmetry with a straightedge and diagonal measurements before welding.
8. Confirm nearby body mount, bracket, and hard-line/fuel/brake routing are not disturbed.

Stop and expand the repair if the metal around the notch is thin, cracked, delaminated, or previously patched poorly.

## Fabrication Method

Preferred repair:

1. Trim only enough jagged metal to reach clean, sound steel with smooth radiused corners.
2. Form a matching mild-steel insert from the measured chassis thickness, including the lower side-wall curve/return profile if present.
3. Use a butt-welded patch where the rail wall is simple and access is good.
4. Use an internal backing sleeve or shaped internal doubler only if needed for alignment or strength. If used, plug-weld it and protect the closed cavity afterward.
5. Avoid square-ended external fishplates unless a chassis fabricator specifically releases reinforcement. If reinforcement is needed, use tapered/radiused ends so stress is not concentrated.
6. Tack the insert with the rail fixed in its natural position, then stitch-weld in short alternating runs to control heat and distortion.
7. Grind only enough to remove sharp/high weld profile. Do not thin the surrounding chassis wall chasing a cosmetic finish.
8. Reopen or preserve any drain, bolt, clip, or bracket clearance that existed in the original shape.

The welder should be a competent chassis/structural automotive welder. This area should not be treated as thin body-panel work.

## Exhaust Reroute Gate

The exhaust must be mocked up after the rail shape is restored.

Requirements:

1. No exhaust pipe should pass through, touch, or require a new notch in the chassis rail/extension.
2. Target at least `25-30 mm` clearance to painted chassis/body surfaces wherever possible.
3. Use larger clearance and/or heat shielding near brake lines, fuel lines, rubber mounts, wiring, bushings, and paint-sensitive surfaces.
4. Check clearance at engine movement, suspension droop/compression, steering movement, axle movement, and body/tub refit position.
5. Add or repair exhaust hanger tabs before final coating if any welded tabs are required.
6. Use proper rubber exhaust hangers so pipe movement is controlled and cannot beat against the repaired rail.

If the new exhaust route needs a hanger, bracket, or heat shield mount on the chassis, include it in the existing pre-coating bracket release workflow.

## Coating And Corrosion Protection

After welding and inspection:

1. Remove weld scale and spatter.
2. Inspect welds for undercut, porosity, pinholes, missed edges, or distortion.
3. Use dye penetrant or another crack check if available, especially at the old notch ends.
4. Epoxy-prime bare steel inside and outside before seam sealing.
5. Seam-seal only after primer where the coating system requires it.
6. Apply Raptor/top protection only after the repair is signed off.
7. Cavity-wax the inside/overlap/backing area last, after primer/top protection has cured.
8. Keep drains, bolt holes, threads, and ground points open and serviceable.

## Acceptance Criteria

The repair is released only when:

- The lower rail/extension profile matches the known-good side or verified reference.
- There are no jagged cut edges, unsealed lap traps, open pinholes, undercut welds, or visible cracks.
- Rail straightness and local bracket/body-mount alignment are unchanged.
- The nearby body mount, crossmember/bracket, lines, and clips still fit and remain serviceable.
- The exhaust mock-up clears the restored rail without contact through expected movement.
- Welded hanger or heat-shield tabs, if required, are finished before final coating.
- Bare metal is epoxy-primed, protected, and cavity-waxed in the correct order.
