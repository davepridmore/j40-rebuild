# Vitz/Yaris EPS Conversion Fabrication Plan

Date: 2026-05-12
Scope: planning and fabrication controls for fitting a 2005-2011 Toyota Vitz/Yaris 90-series column-assist EPS unit into the J40.

## Core Decision

This is a column-assist EPS graft. It is not a hydraulic steering conversion and does not require power-steering pumps, hydraulic boxes, hoses, crank pulleys, or engine removal.

Preferred route: retain as much of the J40 upper-column driving position and dash ergonomics as practical, then package the Vitz/Yaris EPS assist unit lower in the column path with a fabricated support and controlled intermediate shaft.

Do not buy or install a bare EPS column. The matched EPS ECU/controller or seller-proven integrated control, plugs/pigtails, shafts, U-joints, couplers, brackets, support plates, and labels remain required.

## 1) Layout And Measurement

Measure and photograph before cutting:

- J40 steering-wheel height and reach.
- Existing upper-column angle.
- Dash hole and lower dash support position.
- Pedal clearance through full pedal travel.
- Firewall pass-through center and available reinforcement area.
- Steering-box input shaft position and angle.
- Donor EPS motor/gearbox envelope.
- Donor input/output shaft length, spline or DD form, and bracket spacing.
- Proposed EPS ECU/controller mounting envelope.

Hold point: no irreversible column cutting, shaft cutting, drilling, or welding until the centerline, motor envelope, and shaft path are mocked up and photographed.

## 2) Column Support Fabrication

The EPS motor creates torque reaction. The support must carry that load through strong structure, not thin dash sheet alone.

Fabricate or adapt:

- Triangulated under-dash/cowl/firewall column cradle.
- Column clamp or saddle around the EPS body where the donor bracket allows it.
- Firewall/cowl doubler or spreader plate where sheet metal receives load.
- Serviceable fastener access for later column removal.
- Clearance cutouts only where they do not weaken steering support.

Material starting point:

- 3 mm mild steel bracket stock or equivalent.
- M8/M10 class 8.8 fasteners where structural fastening is needed.
- Welded tabs only after full dry-fit, if bolted support alone is not adequate.

Reject design if the motor can rock the column, if support depends on one thin sheet-metal flange, or if the bracket blocks pedals, knees, heater/dash service, wiring, or column removal.

## 3) Shaft, U-Joint And Coupler Adaptation

The steering shaft path is safety-critical.

Required controls:

- Lay out all donor shaft sections, J40 shaft ends, U-joints, couplers, and pinch bolts together.
- Identify spline count/diameter or DD dimensions before machining.
- Keep adequate shaft engagement at every coupler.
- Phase U-joints correctly.
- Check angles lock-to-lock before final fastening.
- Use machined adapters or correct splined/DD couplers.
- Use pinch bolts with proper grooves where the joint design requires them.
- Paint-mark final fasteners after torque.

Do not rely on casual welded steering-shaft joins. Any shaft welding or adapter machining must be treated as specialist work and inspected before road use.

Hold point: the system must steer manually with EPS off, without binding, sliding, or shaft play.

## 4) EPS ECU/Controller And Wiring

The J40 has no vehicle ECU, but the donor EPS still needs its matched EPS ECU/controller unless the column is proven integrated.

Required installation:

- Mount controller in a dry, supported, serviceable cabin location.
- Keep it away from foot impact, water paths, heater leaks, and sharp brackets.
- Use donor pigtails only for connector identification and bench testing.
- Build final power, ground, ignition trigger, fuse protection, and loom with new automotive cable and terminals.
- Protect every pass-through with a grommet or bulkhead fitting.
- Label EPS fuse, trigger feed, ground, and controller connector.
- Photograph the wiring before wrapping or tying the loom closed.

Hold point: no permanent loom closure until powered assist works and voltage drop/ground integrity are checked.

## 5) Validation Before Road Use

Static checks:

- Front axle safely unloaded.
- Steering lock-to-lock with EPS off.
- Steering lock-to-lock with EPS powered.
- No shaft bind, joint notchiness, motor rocking, or wiring movement.
- Pedals, knees, dash, heater, and wiring clear through full movement.
- Firewall seal/grommet fitted.
- Bracket fasteners, pinch bolts, couplers, controller mount, ground, and fuse paint-marked.

Low-speed checks:

- Yard turn test.
- Steering return behavior.
- Braking while turning.
- Recheck bracket movement, shaft play, and wiring after the first movement.

Road release:

- Alignment complete.
- No steering bind or unpredictable assist.
- Manual fallback behavior understood.
- Recheck after short drive, then again after normal road validation.
