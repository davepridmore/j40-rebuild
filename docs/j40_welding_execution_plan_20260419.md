## Chassis Post-Body-Off Task List (Add To Tracker)

### Execution Sequence

1. Dry clean + degrease:
- scrape heavy mud/oil deposits
- degrease rails/crossmembers/body-mount pedestals and line brackets
2. Mechanical rust cleanup:
- wire cup first on non-flat sections (brackets, corners, gussets, curves), then strip/flap cleanup on flatter faces
- wire-brush/strip to solid bright metal in every suspect zone
- stop grinding where section thickness is at risk; log for patch decision instead
3. Defect logging pass (photo + tag each finding):
- rails/crossmembers
- spring hangers/shackle hangers
- steering-box mount zone
- body-mount pedestals and captive threads
- brake/fuel line clips and brackets
4. Repair/approval gate:
- patch or reinforce only verified weak zones
- close all unknowns before coating
5. Protection stack on accepted metal:
- epoxy primer
- seam/joint protection where needed
- chassis topcoat/liner and cavity wax for closed sections

### Issues To Explicitly Check and Add If Found

- Crack lines around steering box mount and front spring hanger areas.
- Deep pitting at top/inside rail faces around body-mount locations.
- Crossmember end thinning near drain/mud-trap zones.
- Body-mount captive nuts with stripped threads, spinning cages, or distorted sleeves.
- Brake/fuel hard-line corrosion under clips and at bend points.
- Missing/weak frame-to-body and engine-to-chassis ground points after cleanup.

## Body-Lift Runbook (Next Steps)

### Lift-Day Checklist (Before First Lift)

1. Confirm all body-to-frame interfaces are free:
- body-mount bolts and captured mounts loosened/removed
- steering linkage/shaft and any column/frame coupling disconnected
- firewall-to-chassis wiring, ground straps, and loom clips released
- brake/clutch/fuel/vent/return lines that bridge body and frame disconnected or safely rerouted
2. Prepare controlled lift hardware:
- 4 lifters at stable corner positions plus 1 dedicated spotter/commander
- timber/cribbing and fixed stands pre-positioned before lifting
- no person allowed under shell during any unsupported movement
3. Perform a test lift only (`20-30 mm`):
- stop and do a full snag walkaround
- only continue when no line/loom/hose/bolt bind is present

### Lift Execution

1. Lift in short synchronized increments, pausing every small height step.
2. Keep shell level; if one side drags, lower and clear the snag before retry.
3. Move shell to prepared stands/cribbing and verify stable support at all corners.

### Immediate Post-Lift (Same Day)

1. Capture measurement/photo set before cleanup:
- each body mount point (body + frame side)
- frame rails/crossmembers and rust boundaries
- underside routing zones that need weld-heat protection
2. Freeze weld zone boundaries for WZ-01/WZ-02/WZ-03 with paint marker and photos.
3. Open chassis track in parallel:
- rail/crossmember inspection list
- repair/no-repair decision log by location

### 24-48 Hour Work Sequence

1. Complete rust-map signoff with cut lines and patch templates.
2. Execute welding in the existing sequence (cut -> fit -> stitch -> verify pinholes).
3. Close same-window corrosion stack on each completed zone (primer -> seam sealer -> topcoat/liner -> cavity wax).

### No-Go Conditions (Delay Lift Until Closed)

- Any still-attached line/loom/shaft between body and frame.
- Missing stable cribbing/stands for immediate shell support.
- No active fire watch + extinguisher coverage during post-lift hot work.

## Weld Zones (Vehicle-Specific)

### WZ-01: Rear-right lower seam strip perforation

- Location: lower seam where the rear floor/inner side structure meets the lower return edge (right-rear side).
- Evidence: `20260413_005516.jpg`, `20260413_005523.jpg`.
- Condition: through-rust and edge perforation across a continuous seam section.
- Repair method:
  - Cut back to bright steel with full perforation removal.
  - Fabricate `1.0-1.2 mm` patch strip matching original profile.
  - Prefer butt-weld where possible; use short stitch sequence to control heat.
  - Recreate factory seam strength with plug welds where original spot-weld flanges were removed.

### WZ-02: Right-rear inner corner rust pocket (adjacent bracket/shelf area)

- Location: right rear inner corner transition near bracketed structure and floor return lip.
- Evidence: `20260405_234652.jpg`.
- Condition: localized heavy scale and pitting with likely hidden backside rust.
- Repair method:
  - Open/grind until full rust boundary is visible.
  - Remove weak lip sections and fabricate localized corner patch.
  - If lip thickness is compromised, add formed reinforcement section before final seam closure.

### WZ-03: Rear tank bay/floor contamination zone

- Location: rear floor area under/around tank seating zone.
- Evidence: `20260413_005435.jpg` plus current workshop photos with tank out.
- Condition: contamination, surface corrosion, and probable seep history from tank area.
- Repair method:
  - Degrease and clean to bare inspection condition.
  - Probe for pinholes using light/air test after cleaning.
  - Patch only where perforated; treat and seal non-perforated rust.
  - Keep drain/inspection accessibility for future tank service.

### WZ-04: Underside protection while welding

- Location: underside areas with routed lines/loom near floor seams.
- Evidence: `20260331_224409.jpg` (context only; several underside shots are motion-blurred and need re-shoot before final signoff).
- Method:
  - Pull/clip away lines and temporary loom at least `150 mm` from active weld heat zones.
  - Install weld blanket shielding and fire watch under every active seam section.

## Execution Sequence (No Rework Order)

1. **Safety isolate**
- Remove tank from work zone; cap all fuel ports.
- Remove/relocate nearby rubber hoses, loose loom, and flammables.
- Keep extinguisher + water + fire watch active during welding and 30 minutes after.

2. **Rust-map mark-up on shell**
- Mark WZ-01 and WZ-02 cut boundaries with paint marker.
- Photograph each marked zone before cutting.

3. **Cut and fabricate**
- Cut only fully perforated/soft metal first.
- Template with cardboard, then transfer to `1.0-1.2 mm` sheet.
- Trial fit with maximum `1 mm` gap.

4. **Weld in controlled passes**
- Tack every `25-30 mm`, then alternate stitch pattern.
- No long continuous bead on floor sheet.
- Cool between passes to avoid warping.

5. **Dress and verify**
- Grind flush only where needed for panel fit/seal.
- Re-check with light from underside for pinholes.
- Re-weld any pinholes before coating.

6. **Immediate corrosion stack (same work window)**
- Clean/degrease.
- Epoxy primer on bare metal.
- Seam sealer on all repaired joints/laps.
- Top protective coat/liner.
- Cavity wax in closed seams/cavities after primer/paint cure.

7. **Pre-refit gate**
- Refit tank only after all weld zones are closed, primed, seam-sealed, and dry.
- Perform leak check and hose replacement while tank is still accessible.

## Quality Gates (Pass/Fail)

- All perforation removed; no rust islands left at cut edge.
- No visible pinholes after backlight test.
- No weld-through to nearby lines/loom; no heat damage on adjacent components.
- Coating stack completed in order: primer -> seam sealer -> topcoat/liner -> cavity wax.
- Photo evidence captured per zone: pre-cut, post-weld, post-coating.
