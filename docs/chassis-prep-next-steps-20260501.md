Use [chassis-bracket-audit-20260508.md](chassis-bracket-audit-20260508.md) as the bracket closure sheet.

The correct approach is not to strip every sound black coating to bare metal. Keep hard-bonded paint where it is solid, feather its edges, and focus effort on loose rust, pitting, seams, holes, brackets, and places where water or dirt can sit.

## Best Overall Approach

1. Work by zone, not randomly across the frame.
2. Dry clean and blow/vacuum dust before using any wet cleaner.
3. Use wire cup and hand wire brushes on non-flat geometry.
4. Use strip disc or flap disc only on flatter rail faces where there is control; these are angle-grinder consumables, and the existing flap/strip stock should be physically counted before rebuying.
5. Do not thin bracket edges, hanger roots, or pitted rail lips chasing perfect shine.
6. Move hard-line clips only where needed and safe; inspect under the clip contact points before coating.
7. Degrease only after the second brushing pass, otherwise oil and dust hide defects.
8. Rinse with controlled pressure, dry fully, then inspect with bright light.
9. Treat only remaining rust in pits/seams; do not apply converter to clean steel.
10. Close the welded-bracket work plan before final primer/Raptor: analysis register, design release, implementation, and validation, especially radiator, battery holder, auxiliary tabs, and exhaust hanger/mock-up points.
11. Prime only after repair decisions and signoff photos are complete.

### Work Instructions

1. Blow out the whole rail section with air or brush/vacuum first.
2. Mark every body-mount pad with tape or chalk so the worker knows these are inspection points, not just paint areas.
3. Use a small wire cup on the rail top flange, lower flange, crossmember junctions, and bracket roots.
4. Use hand wire brush or small rotary brush inside holes and tight corners.
5. Feather old black coating edges until they do not lift under a scraper.
6. Do not grind deeply into pitted metal; if pitting remains after loose rust is removed, log it and treat the pits.
7. Chase or test every captive thread before primer. Do not fill threads with primer.
8. Take close-up signoff photos of each mount pad and bracket root after final dry brushing.

Acceptance before moving on: no loose coating, no loose rust, no dust packed into holes, bracket roots visible, body-mount pads flat enough to inspect.

### Work Instructions

1. Start with the steering-box mount side. This is a structural hold point.
2. Clean the steering-box mount, nearby rail face, bolt holes, and hanger weld toes to inspection condition.
3. Use wire cup on bracket roots; use hand brush around lines and steering parts.
4. Inspect for crack lines radiating from bolt holes, weld ends, and hanger roots.
5. Check for ovalized holes, distorted brackets, or thin metal before primer.
6. If any crack or elongation is visible, stop coating in that zone and mark it for repair/reinforcement.
7. Clean bumper/winch brackets separately; dirt often remains behind bolt heads, plates, and lap joints.
8. Do not seal bracket laps until they are dry and inspected.

Acceptance before primer: steering-box mount photo-signed, front hanger roots photo-signed, no hidden rust left at front horn/crossmember joints, repair decisions marked.

## Exact Workshop Sequence

### Pass 1 - Dry Detail Prep

Use this pass before any wet cleaning.

1. Blow/vacuum the chassis.
2. Scrape heavy mud, old sealer lumps, and loose coating.
3. Wire-cup:
   - rail top/lower flanges
   - body-mount pads
   - spring hanger roots
   - steering-box mount area
   - crossmember ends
   - bumper/winch brackets
4. Hand-brush:
   - hard-line clips
   - bolt holes
   - bracket inside corners
   - wiring/line-adjacent areas
5. Feather sound paint edges; do not chase bare metal where coating is solid.

### Pass 2 - Inspection Prep

1. Wipe dust off with dry cloth.
2. Use bright light and pick/probe on:
   - steering-box mount
   - front and rear hanger roots
   - crossmember ends
   - body mount pads
   - rail lower edges
   - under hard-line clips
3. Mark every missing, broken, wire-tied, or non-baseline bracket/tab with paint marker/tape.
4. Separate required baseline brackets from optional additions before welding.
5. Take close photos before repair or coating.

### Pass 3 - Degrease And Rinse

Use the products already logged:

- `DISS/APC cleaner`: broad dust and grime.
- `GREZ OFF`: oily/greasy chassis spots only.
- `Wadfow WRS1550 pressure sprayer`: apply diluted cleaner; it is not a powered pressure washer.

Method:

1. Apply cleaner from bottom upward to avoid dry streaks.
2. Agitate with brushes.
3. Rinse with controlled pressure.
4. Do not aim water directly into:
   - brake drums/backing plates
   - axle seals
   - wheel bearings
   - breathers
   - electrical connectors
   - open lines
   - steering seals
5. Blow dry with compressed air, especially seams, bolt holes, clips, and boxed areas.
6. Leave the chassis to dry fully before chemical treatment or primer.

### Pass 4 - Rust Treatment

Use rust chemistry only after dry inspection.

1. Clean bare steel with no rust: no converter needed.
2. Pitted steel with rust remaining: use compatible converter only in pits/seams.
3. Removable small parts: Evapo-Rust is acceptable if the part can stay wet/immersed properly.
4. Remove residue according to product instructions.
5. Do not trap converter residue under epoxy primer.

### Pass 5 - Primer And Protection

Only start this after the hold points below are closed.

1. Wax and grease remover wipe.
2. Mask threads, ground pads, brake/fuel line fittings, and rubber parts. Use sacrificial bolts for threaded holes; the on-hand grommet pack can be used as temporary open-hole masking only where each piece fits snugly and survives a solvent test.
3. Apply the selected zinc-rich 2K epoxy primer to approved metal.
4. Seam sealer only after primer where product system requires it.
5. Apply the on-hand U-POL/Raptor protective coating as the exposed chassis top protection after primer/sealer cure.
6. Apply HB Body U900 cavity wax spray cans last inside hidden/boxed/lapped sections with the wand/nozzle; do not block drains, threads, bolt holes, or future ground points.
7. Reopen ground pads to clean metal during final electrical ground installation, then protect around the completed connection.
