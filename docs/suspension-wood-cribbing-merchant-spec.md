# Suspension Wood Cribbing Merchant Spec

Give this to the timber/wood merchant for the suspension support blocks.

## Order

Supply **12 total pieces** of dry, solid hardwood:

| Qty | Piece | Size |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 8 | Rectangular cribbing/support blocks | **12 in x 6 in x 3 in** each, approx **300 x 150 x 75 mm** |
| 4 | Wedge chocks | Cut from **8 in x 4 in x 3 in** blanks, approx **200 x 100 x 75 mm**, tapering lengthwise from **75 mm** rear height to **20-25 mm** nose height |

## Wood Requirement

Use seasoned solid hardwood only, such as **sheesham/shisham**, **kikar/acacia**, **oak**, **ash**, or equivalent dense hardwood.

Do not supply pine, deodar/chir, poplar, plywood, MDF, chipboard, laminated/glued board, fresh/wet wood, painted/oily offcuts, cracked timber, split pieces, or pieces with large knots on the bearing faces.

## Cutting Requirement

- Cut the rectangular blocks with flat, parallel faces and square ends.
- Run the grain along the long dimension of each piece.
- Leave the wood raw and unfinished.
- Lightly chamfer sharp edges by 3-5 mm if possible, but keep the broad bearing faces flat.
- Pieces should sit flat without rocking.

If the merchant cannot cut the wedge chocks cleanly, buy **4 extra 8 in x 4 in x 3 in solid hardwood blocks** and let the workshop cut the taper.

## Use Note

These blocks are for cribbing and stabilising during the Ironman suspension replacement. They supplement rated jack stands and axle support; they are not a substitute for rated stands.
