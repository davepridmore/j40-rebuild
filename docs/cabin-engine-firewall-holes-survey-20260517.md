# Cabin-to-Engine Firewall Holes Survey - 2026-05-17

Evidence source: Google Photos picker run `20260517T233950`.

This survey belongs to the electrical refit because the existing cabin-to-engine openings determine where the loom, diesel cutoff/control wiring, sender wiring, and any future service pass-throughs can safely run. The photos show existing openings with tape-measure references, old sealant, paint loss, rust staining, and steering-column/boot context.

## Rule

No new wire or control cable goes through bare sheet metal. Every reused hole needs a measured diameter, clean edge, grommet or bulkhead fitting, strain relief, water/fume sealing, and clearance from steering and pedals.

## Refit Decisions

- Prefer existing holes only if they are round, clean, measurable, and can take a proper grommet or bulkhead fitting.
- Plug unused holes with sealed blanking plugs where sheet metal is sound.
- Repair or weld-close distorted, jagged, or rust-thinned openings before paint and final insulation.
- Keep wiring away from the steering column boot, pedal movement, sharp brackets, and old screw/fastener edges.
- Do not finalize loom wrap until the pass-through route is chosen and the Electrical Master is updated.

## Verification Checklist

- Measure each opening with calipers, not only the tape in the photo.
- Photograph both cabin side and engine side for every candidate hole.
- Deburr and rust-treat any reused hole edge.
- Match grommet inside diameter to bundle size and outside groove to firewall thickness.
- Confirm bend radius and strain relief on both sides.
- Seal against water, dust, and exhaust/fuel fumes.
- Check steering and pedal sweep after temporary loom placement.
- Record final status as `reuse`, `plug`, `repair`, or `close`.
